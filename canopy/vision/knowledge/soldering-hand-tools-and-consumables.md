# Soldering hand tools and consumables

> tags: soldering, iron, flux, solder, wick, ipa, consumables, hand-rework, workmanship

Most repair quality is determined before the solder melts: tip choice, flux choice, thermal mass, cleanliness, component support, and inspection.

## Core tools
- Temperature-controlled soldering station with interchangeable tips.
- Fine conical/hoof tips for small work, chisel tips for thermal mass, bevel tips for drag soldering.
- Quality flux: no-clean, rosin/RMA, or water-soluble depending on process and cleaning ability.
- Solder wire matched to the board alloy and repair policy.
- Solder wick, solder sucker, low-melt alloy for removal when appropriate, tweezers, ESD brush, and 99% IPA or qualified cleaner.
- Microscope or at least 10–30× magnification.

## Practical soldering rules
- Use the largest tip that fits the joint; too small a tip overheats the part because dwell time becomes long.
- Add flux before reflow. Flux fixes wetting; extra heat does not.
- Heat both pad and lead, feed solder to the joint, not only to the iron.
- Avoid prying components while solder is partially solid; lifted pads are usually mechanical damage plus heat.
- Clean and inspect after rework, especially under low-standoff parts and around fine-pitch leads.

## Good joint clues
- Smooth, wetted, concave fillet.
- Lead and pad both visibly wetted.
- No cracks, rings, dull/grainy cold joints, bridges, solder balls, or disturbed surfaces.
- No excessive solder hiding lead geometry or wetting defects.

## Flux and residue
- No-clean flux is not a license to leave heavy hand-rework residue in harsh environments.
- Water-soluble flux must be thoroughly removed; residue can be conductive/corrosive.
- In automotive, appliance, outdoor, humid, or high-impedance analog boards, cleaning matters because leakage can mimic component failure.

## Rework discipline
- One repair action at a time; inspect and re-measure after each.
- Protect nearby plastic connectors, electrolytics, MEMS sensors, relays, labels, and conformal coating.
- Replace lost coating/insulation after rework.
- Log tip temperature, method, part replaced, and inspection outcome for repeat jobs.
